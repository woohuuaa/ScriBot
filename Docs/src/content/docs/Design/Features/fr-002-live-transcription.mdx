## Overview

Live Transcription converts spoken parliamentary debate audio into text in real-time using advanced
AI speech recognition technology, enabling immediate accessibility and searchability of proceedings
as they happen.

## Actors

- **Department Staff**: Monitor live transcription to identify questions directed at their ministry
  during debates
- **Transcription Editor**: Review and correct AI-generated transcripts for accuracy
- **Department Coordinator**: Oversee transcript monitoring across multiple debates simultaneously
- **System Administrator**: Configure and monitor transcription services
- **Transcription Service**: AI-powered speech recognition engine processing audio

## Use Cases

### UC-001: Monitor Live Debate Transcription

**Actor**: Department Staff  
**Description**: Watch real-time transcription during live parliamentary debate to identify
questions directed at their ministry, enabling rapid response preparation while proceedings
continue.

### UC-002: Identify Speakers for Question Attribution

**Actor**: Transcription Editor  
**Description**: Automatically identify and label different speakers in the transcription output,
maintaining clear attribution of statements to specific parliament members for accurate question
tracking.

### UC-003: Handle Multi-language Parliamentary Sessions

**Actor**: System Administrator  
**Description**: Process debates that include multiple languages (primarily Dutch, with occasional
English), automatically detecting language changes and maintaining transcription accuracy across
language boundaries.

### UC-004: Correct Transcription Errors

**Actor**: Transcription Editor  
**Description**: Review and correct AI transcription errors in technical terminology, proper nouns,
or specialized policy language to ensure accurate records for question extraction and response
preparation.

## Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Transcribing Multi-Speaker Committee Debate

**Preconditions**:

- Audio stream is actively being processed from FR-001
- Transcription service is initialized with parliamentary vocabulary
- Multiple speakers are participating in the debate
- User interface is ready to display real-time output

**Steps**:

1. First speaker begins addressing the committee
2. Audio segments are received from stream processing pipeline
3. Speech recognition engine processes audio in real-time
4. Speaker diarization identifies the active speaker
5. Transcribed text appears on screen with speaker label
6. Second speaker interjects with a question
7. System detects speaker change and updates label
8. Transcription continues with new speaker attribution
9. Text is formatted with proper punctuation and timestamps
10. Both speakers' statements are clearly differentiated in output

**Postconditions**:

- Complete transcription of both speakers is available
- Speaker changes are accurately detected and labeled
- Text is properly formatted and timestamped
- Transcription is stored for later review and question extraction

### Scenario 2: Processing Debate with Challenging Audio Conditions

**Preconditions**:

- Parliamentary session is in progress with active transcription
- Audio quality begins to degrade due to technical issues
- Multiple staff members are monitoring the transcription
- Background noise increases due to chamber activity

**Steps**:

1. Transcription is proceeding normally with good accuracy
2. Audio quality degradation is detected by the system
3. Noise reduction algorithms are automatically applied
4. Transcription confidence scores begin to decrease
5. System flags low-confidence segments for review
6. Transcription continues with best-effort processing
7. Quality indicator shows "Reduced Accuracy" to users
8. Staff are notified of audio quality issues
9. Audio quality improves as technical issue is resolved
10. System returns to normal accuracy mode
11. Flagged segments remain marked for post-session review

**Postconditions**:

- Continuous transcription maintained throughout incident
- Low-confidence segments are flagged for manual review
- Users were informed of temporary quality reduction
- Complete record exists despite audio challenges

### Scenario 3: Handling Language Switching in International Debate

**Preconditions**:

- Parliamentary debate includes international participants
- Transcription service supports both Dutch and English
- Multiple languages are expected during the session
- Automatic language detection is enabled

**Steps**:

1. Dutch speaker begins presentation to the chamber
2. System processes speech as Dutch language
3. Transcription appears with appropriate Dutch grammar rules
4. Speaker concludes and English-speaking guest is introduced
5. System detects language change to English
6. Transcription seamlessly switches language models
7. English transcription appears with correct language formatting
8. Debate returns to Dutch for question period
9. System switches back to Dutch language processing
10. All transcription segments are labeled with detected language

**Postconditions**:

- Both Dutch and English segments are accurately transcribed
- Language changes are detected and handled automatically
- Each segment is labeled with its language
- No loss of content during language transitions

## Technical Overview

### High-Level Architecture

The transcription system integrates multiple AI components into a real-time processing pipeline:

**Speech Recognition Engine**: Converts audio segments into text using trained language models
optimized for Dutch parliamentary language. Processes audio in small chunks to minimize latency
while maintaining context across segments.

**Speaker Diarization**: Analyzes audio characteristics to identify distinct speakers, assigning
consistent labels throughout the session and detecting speaker changes in real-time.

**Text Processing Pipeline**: Applies natural language processing to add proper punctuation,
capitalization, and formatting. Includes custom vocabulary for parliamentary terms, political names,
and technical terminology.

**Quality Assurance Layer**: Monitors transcription confidence scores, detects potential errors, and
flags low-confidence segments for manual review. Adapts processing based on audio quality
conditions.

**Output Management**: Formats transcribed text with timestamps, speaker labels, and metadata.
Delivers output via WebSocket for real-time display and stores in database for persistence.

### Key Design Principles

- **Real-time First**: Optimized for minimal latency over perfect accuracy
- **Graceful Degradation**: Maintains service during quality challenges
- **Context Awareness**: Leverages debate context for accuracy improvement
- **Observable Quality**: Provides confidence indicators to users
