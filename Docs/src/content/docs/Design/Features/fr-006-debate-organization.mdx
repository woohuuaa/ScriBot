## Overview

Debate Organization provides comprehensive tools for organizing parliamentary debates by multiple
dimensions including rooms, dates, departments, and topics, enabling efficient navigation,
management, and discovery of parliamentary proceedings across the organization.

## Actors

- **Information Manager**: Organizes and categorizes debates with comprehensive metadata
- **Department Staff**: Searches debates relevant to their ministry
- **Department Coordinator**: Views debates involving their ministry and tracks participation
- **System Administrator**: Configures organizational taxonomy and structure
- **Policy Officer**: Links related debate sessions and maintains relationships

## Use Cases

### UC-001: Categorize New Debate Session

**Actor**: Information Manager  
**Description**: Assign comprehensive metadata to newly created debate including room, date,
participating ministries, primary topics, and related legislation to ensure proper organization and
ministry staff can discover relevant debates.

### UC-002: Search Debates by Ministry and Topic

**Actor**: Department Staff  
**Description**: Search and filter debates by topic area, ministry involvement, and time period to
locate debates where questions were directed to their ministry.

### UC-003: Track Ministry-Specific Debates

**Actor**: Department Coordinator  
**Description**: View all debates involving a specific ministry, filter by topic and date range, and
monitor where the ministry was questioned or discussed in parliamentary proceedings.

### UC-004: Link Related Debate Sessions

**Actor**: Policy Officer  
**Description**: Establish relationships between related debates such as follow-up sessions on the
same topic or debates referencing common legislation to support contextual understanding for
response preparation.

## Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Organizing Multi-Department Climate Policy Debate

**Preconditions**:

- New debate session has been created in system
- Multiple ministries are involved in climate policy discussion
- Topic taxonomy includes climate-related categories
- Room and date information is available

**Steps**:

1. Information Manager opens new debate details page
2. Manager assigns debate to "Main Chamber" room
3. Manager sets date to current session date
4. Manager begins assigning ministries involved
5. Manager selects "Ministry of Climate and Energy" as primary ministry
6. Manager adds "Ministry of Finance" as secondary ministry
7. Manager adds "Ministry of Transport" as tertiary ministry
8. Manager navigates to topic classification section
9. Manager selects primary topic: "Climate Policy > Emissions Reduction"
10. Manager adds secondary topic: "Budget > Climate Funding"
11. Manager enters debate description and legislative context
12. Manager links related legislation: "Climate Act 2024"
13. System validates all required metadata is complete
14. Manager saves debate organization metadata
15. Debate appears in searches for all three ministries and both topics

**Postconditions**:

- Debate is properly categorized across multiple dimensions
- Ministry staff can find debate through multiple search paths
- Department coordinators can track their ministry's involvement
- Topic-based research includes this debate

### Scenario 2: Finding Debates on Healthcare Budget for Ministry Briefing

**Preconditions**:

- Department staff needs historical context on healthcare funding questions
- Debate archive exists spanning recent years
- Search and filter systems are operational
- Topic taxonomy includes healthcare and budget categories

**Steps**:

1. Department Staff member opens debate search interface
2. Staff selects "Browse by Topic" navigation
3. Staff expands "Health Policy" topic category
4. Staff selects "Healthcare Funding" sub-topic
5. System displays 127 debates tagged with this topic
6. Staff applies ministry filter: "Ministry of Health"
7. Results narrow to 89 debates
8. Staff applies date range filter: "Last 5 Years"
9. Results narrow to 23 relevant debates
10. Staff sorts results by date: newest first
11. Staff reviews debate titles and descriptions
12. Staff selects most relevant debate for detailed review
13. Staff views related debates shown in sidebar
14. Staff exports list of all 23 debates for briefing preparation

**Postconditions**:

- Staff has located relevant historical debates
- Response preparation is supported by organized historical data
- Related debates provide additional context
- Search results can be referenced in ministry briefing

### Scenario 3: Tracking Debate Series Across Parliamentary Session

**Preconditions**:

- Ongoing legislative process requires multiple debate sessions
- First debate session completed last month
- Second debate scheduled for today
- Policy Officer tracks debate relationships

**Steps**:

1. Policy Officer opens completed first debate
2. Officer reviews debate metadata and content
3. Officer navigates to "Related Debates" section
4. Officer clicks "Add Related Debate"
5. Officer searches for today's debate session
6. Officer selects relationship type: "Sequel - Continuation"
7. Officer adds note: "Continuation of legislative review process"
8. System creates bidirectional relationship link
9. Today's debate now shows previous debate as "Predecessor"
10. Previous debate shows today's debate as "Sequel"
11. Users viewing either debate see the relationship
12. Timeline view shows debates in sequence
13. Third debate is scheduled and will be linked similarly

**Postconditions**:

- Debate series is linked and trackable
- Ministry staff can navigate between related debates
- Legislative process timeline is clear
- Contextual understanding is enhanced for response preparation

## Technical Overview

### High-Level Architecture

The debate organization system provides flexible multi-dimensional classification:

**Classification System**: Debates can be organized across multiple orthogonal dimensions:
physical/virtual rooms, dates and time periods, ministries/departments, and hierarchical topic
taxonomy. Each dimension is independently searchable and filterable.

**Metadata Management**: Comprehensive metadata schema captures debate context including basic
information (title, description, date), location (room, chamber), participants (ministries,
speakers), topics (primary and secondary), and legislative context (related bills and acts).

**Relationship Graph**: Debates can be linked through various relationship types
(sequel/continuation, related topic, cross-reference, response/rebuttal). Relationships are
bidirectional and navigable from either debate.

**Search and Discovery**: Multi-dimensional search supports filtering across all organization
dimensions simultaneously. Full-text search combined with metadata filters enables precise debate
location. Faceted search shows available filter options with result counts.

**Taxonomy Management**: Flexible topic taxonomy allows hierarchical organization of subject areas.
Topics can be added and reorganized as parliamentary priorities evolve. Support for cross-cutting
topics that span multiple ministries.

### Data Model

```
Debate → Room, Date, Duration
      → Departments (many-to-many)
      → Topics (many-to-many with hierarchy)
      → Related Debates (many-to-many with relationship type)
      → Transcripts (one-to-many)
      → Questions (one-to-many)
```

### Key Design Principles

- **Multi-dimensional**: Support multiple orthogonal classification dimensions
- **Flexible**: Adaptable taxonomy and relationship types
- **Discoverable**: Multiple pathways to find relevant content
- **Contextual**: Rich metadata provides debate context
- **Scalable**: Efficient for large debate archives
